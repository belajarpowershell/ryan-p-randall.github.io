CRAAP test

  1. Currency
  2. Relevance
  3. Authority
  4. Accuracy
  5. Purpose
Blakeslee, S. (2004). The CRAAP test.

Currency

  1. When was this source published?
  2. Important for timeliness of information.
    3. *RIGHT*

Relevance

  1. Why is this right for this essay?
  2. Important for suitablity of approach and depth for your purposes.
*Right*

Authority

  1. Who wrote or made this?
  2. Important for whether they have appropriate expertise for this subject.
*Right*

Accuracy

  1. How does this source support its claims?
  2. Important so readers can check where the authors are getting their facts and examples.
*Right*

Purpose

  1. Why was this written?
  2. Important so readers can know if the author is trying to benefit personally from this.
*Right*

Evaluating appropriate secondary sources

Secondary sources are:

  • removed from the event by degrees of time or involvement
  • often summarizing or analyzing
  • often journalistic or historical
*RIGHT* They're also often written with journalistic or historical tone.
